One Health

  • One Health Concept: A collaborative approach recognizing the interconnectedness of human, animal, and environmental health.
  • Interconnected Health: Health of humans, animals, and ecosystems are closely linked, and changes in one can affect the others.
  • Zoonotic Diseases: Focuses on preventing the spread of diseases that can be transmitted from animals to humans.
  • Environmental Impact: Addresses environmental risks that may influence public health, including climate change and pollution.
  • Global Health Threats: Recognizes global challenges like climate change, biodiversity loss, and infectious disease spread, and their impacts on health.
  • Collaboration Across Sectors: Involves healthcare providers, veterinarians, environmentalists, and other professionals in tackling health challenges.
  • Sustainable Practices: Promotes sustainable practices that protect ecosystems and improve health outcomes for both humans and animals.
  • Integrated Solutions: Combines research, policy, and collaboration to address health crises and promote overall well-being.
  • Long-Term Health: Aims to improve health outcomes, creating a resilient and healthier world for future generations.
